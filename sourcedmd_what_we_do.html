The Platform

What SourcedMD Actually Is

An AI-powered medical intelligence platform that pulls from peer-reviewed research, clinical literature, billing data, and insurance documentation — and translates it into plain language you can use today.

How It Works

The Process, Step by Step

1

You describe your situation

A diagnosis you don't understand. A bill that looks wrong. A condition you're managing. A treatment you're considering. You type it in plain English — no medical terminology required.

2

We search everything

SourcedMD queries clinical databases, insurance documentation, billing records, and social media simultaneously. Not a Google search. A structured retrieval across verified medical and regulatory sources plus millions of real patient experiences.

PubMed PMC Clinical Guidelines CPT/ICD Codes EOB Data Reddit Patient Forums Facebook Groups TikTok Plan Documents Coverage Language
3

We cross-validate and filter

Social media findings are checked against the clinical literature. Billing anomalies are compared to standard coding practices and your plan's coverage rules. Nothing reaches you unless it's been validated. Every claim is sourced. Every recommendation is cited.

If 10,000 people on Reddit say magnesium helps migraines, we check whether the clinical literature supports it, at what dose, for which migraine type, and with what evidence quality. Then we tell you all of that.
4

You get a plain-language report

Cited sources. Actionable steps. Language you can bring to your doctor, your insurer, or your next appointment. Not a wall of medical jargon — a clear answer with the evidence behind it.

Data Sources

Where the Intelligence Comes From

● Clinical Literature

  • Peer-reviewed research papers (PubMed, PMC)
  • Clinical practice guidelines (AMA, specialty societies)
  • Drug interaction databases
  • Treatment protocol standards
  • Diagnostic criteria (DSM, ICD)
Same sources your doctor uses — but translated so you can actually read them.

● Lived Experience

  • Reddit medical communities (r/askdocs, condition-specific subs)
  • Facebook patient groups
  • TikTok health content (filtered for credibility)
  • Disease-specific forums (PatientsLikeMe, Inspire, HealingWell)
  • Caregiver support communities
Every social finding is validated against clinical evidence before it reaches you.

● Insurance Documentation

  • Plan coverage language and exclusions
  • State and federal coverage mandates
  • Prior authorization requirements
  • Appeal rights and timelines
  • Parity laws and compliance rules
Your insurer wrote these rules. We read them so you know what you're actually owed.

● Billing Intelligence

  • CPT and ICD-10 code validation
  • Facility fee and provider charge analysis
  • Duplicate charge detection
  • Out-of-network balance billing rules
  • Surprise billing protections (No Surprises Act)
Hospital billing departments make mistakes. We find them.
